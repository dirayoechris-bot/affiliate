## What to Look for in Online Yoga Teacher Training

Not all online YTT programs are created equal. Here are the key factors to consider:

### 1. Yoga Alliance Accreditation
Ensure the school is a Registered Yoga School (RYS) with Yoga Alliance. This guarantees the curriculum meets international standards and allows you to register as an RYT-200 upon completion.

### 2. Live Interaction & Community
Look for programs that offer live Zoom calls, mentorship, or community forums. Training from home can feel isolating without real-time connection and support.

### 3. Comprehensive Curriculum
A solid 200-hour program should cover asana, pranayama, meditation, yoga philosophy, anatomy, and teaching methodology — not just physical postures.

### 4. Flexible Structure
Self-paced learning works well for many, but check if there are deadlines or required live sessions that fit your schedule.

### 5. Authentic Teachers
Research the lead instructors’ backgrounds. Are they experienced, E-RYT-500 certified, and do they embody the teachings they share?

### 6. Student Support
Does the program offer feedback on assignments, mentorship, or a clear path to ask questions? Support is crucial when learning complex material from home.

### 7. Reviews & Testimonials
Look for verified reviews with specific outcomes. Real stories from graduates (with names and locations) build trust.

## Is Online Yoga Teacher Training Right for You?

Training from home works best if you:
- Are self-motivated and can create a dedicated practice space
- Value flexibility over rigid schedules
- Seek a program with live elements to combat isolation
- Want to study teachings that resonate with your personal goals (spiritual, therapeutic, or fitness-focused)

It may be challenging if you:
- Require in-person adjustments to feel confident in alignment
- Struggle with self-discipline without external deadlines
- Prefer learning exclusively through immersive, in-person experiences
